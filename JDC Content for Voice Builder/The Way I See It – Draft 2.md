# Begin

Hi friend,

Welcome to my world\!

Of all of the books you could be reading, of all of the things you could be doing, I’m grateful that you’ve landed between these pages. Trust that you are here for a reason. That there’s some greater part of you that has guided you to these pages for *something.* I invite you to turn these pages to go find that something, or perhaps more appropriately – let that something find you. 

I like to consider myself a passionately curious student of life. I’ve always been fascinated by the world around us. Always aiming to learn *something* from *everything*. What can this Uber driver teach me about life? How is this heavy emotion of fear or doubt here to serve me? How is this driver road raging behind me a teacher for me in this moment? How might this challenge be a blessing in disguise? I believe that no one (and no thing) comes into our life by a mere coincidence and that everyone (and every thing) carries a piece of the puzzle.

I’ve found that there’s a shift that happens when you approach life with a sense of curiosity. It creates space for processing. It leaves a breath for consciously responding rather than impulsively reacting. It opens a blank page for you to author the story the way you want to see it. 

Curiosity opens the door for miracles. 

I define a miracle as a shift in perception. When you can see something differently – through the light of love rather than the shadow of fear, through the clarity of truth rather than the foginess of emotion, through the connection of oneness rather than the illusion of separation.

This is a book full of miracles. This is a collection of select **adventures, stories, letters, poems, and photographs** that have shifted my reality, upleveled my perspective, and changed my way of being.

This is The Way I See It. 

I am honored to share this intimate piece of me with you. Let the light shine through and crack the door wide open to the miracles and magic of life as a human being on planet earth.

Oh, and one last disclaimer before we get going…Don’t listen to a word I say.

With all of my love, 

Joshua Dean Church

# My Name is Joshua

Hi, my name is Joshua.

I like being barefoot, having playdates with friends, and biting into a piece of fresh fruit.

I’m a 29 year-old Moroccan, American, Jewish kid from California with the last name Church.

Maybe I’ll become a Rabbi one day. Rabbi Church. Has a nice ring to it.

I love hugs. Good hugs though, not those half-ass-one-arm-side-hugs.

I am a work in progress.

I enjoy being alone with my journal and noise canceling headphones as much as I love watching movies or listening to music that moves my emotions.  

I think my favorite part of being human is experiencing the feeling of laughter to the point of tears. Like where you are doubled over, trying to catch your breath to the point of physical pain. That’s the greatest.

I live for the moments where the dots connect. You know, the ones where you can look back and see exactly why that thing that seemingly happened TO you, really happened FOR you? Ah, those are the best.

My favorite form of feedback is receiving goosebumps.

I enjoy diving into meaningful cosmic bending conversation to the same depth that I despise surface level small talk in the kiddie pool. Life’s too short for small talk.

Life’s ALSO too short to skip dessert.

I had an obsession with rainbow sprinkles when I was younger.

Okay, I HAVE an obsession with rainbow sprinkles, still. 

I feel like life is one big class project and you get to pick your partners. I want to choose mine wisely and be the best classmate I can. So like the opposite of what I was in the 7th grade. 

I sometimes pretend to know the answers when I’m really just trying to figure it out myself.

I love yoga, breathwork, moving my body, and doing things that make sitting on the couch after feel like a well deserved reward.

I also like to take ice baths. Well, I don’t like taking ice baths but I like what they bring me and love the feeling after.

I carry a lot of tension and stress in my shoulders, and notice myself clenching them often. And then I take a deep breath, I love taking a deep breath.

I believe that happiness is an inside job and that we are responsible for our own health, strength, and happiness and that we are far more beautiful, capable, and powerful than we give ourselves credit for.

I live for the moments when I can play a small role in creating the space for healing and transformation to take place.

I think that may be why I’m here this time around.

I’ve never had my heart broken. Maybe I need to open up more. 

I take pictures sometimes, and still tie my shoelaces bunny ear style, all the time.

Giving feels good. Really good. So good that I think that helping others is the whole point of this thing.

In this lifetime, I am blessed.

I love celebrating birthdays and also celebrating when my socks match up after doing laundry.

I like staying at hotels and wearing hotel robes. Not the scratchy kind though.

I love people watching, meeting new friends, and Trader Joes. Sometimes that all happens at Trader Joes. Not all the time though.

I don’t really know what I want to be when I grow up, but I know that it’s going to be the best version of me possible. I’m excited to see what that looks like.

I define success as being able to look back and say with confidence that I did the best that I could with all that I had. I know if I give everyday the full effort it deserves, I will succeed. 

I love palm trees and Sunday Farmers Markets. That deserves its own line.

I don’t like when I’m late and wasting other people’s time because I don’t like when other people are late or waste my time. I’ve never missed a flight. 

I sometimes fear that I won’t make a big enough impact on the world.

I like taking the stairs at the airport. Maybe because of the steps, maybe because I like doing what others aren’t. 

I have to admit, this past year I’ve found myself falling madly in love.

With myself.

Gotta start somewhere, right?

I love the love that I am.  
I am the awareness between the I and the Am.  
I am the space between thinking of these thoughts.  
I am the universe experiencing and perceiving itself.

I sometimes hurt my head thinking deeply like that. Then I go jump in the ocean or hug a tree and everything makes senseless sense again. 

I believe that magic is real. Have you ever seen a rainbow? Or the northern lights? Or stared into a fire losing all perception of time?

How about staring into another person’s eyes until all of time and space and perception melt away?

I really think the big show is happening now. Not tomorrow, not yesterday, not in the next life \- but now\!

One time I cried looking at a flower because it was so beautiful and felt it was placed just right for me. I thought maybe it was because I was smoking too much weed at that time of my life so I stopped smoking. Then I cried when I was on a run and a butterfly landed on my shoulder and ran with me. Maybe it was the endorphins. Maybe, something greater.

I am where I am and that’s okay.

I am a powerful, deliberate co-creator, on the leading edge of reality.

An astronaut exploring outer space.  
And inner space.

I try not to take life too seriously. After all, no one gets out alive anyway. 

It may sound grim to you, but I find tremendous relief in the fact that we all are going to die. 

The problem you are wrestling with right now? Probably not going to be thinking about it on your deathbed. 

We get this one chance of being alive, and the odds are as preposterous that we are here.

We only have a few dozen trips around the sun (if we’re lucky) so I want to soak up as much goodness and let go of as much worry as possible. 

And I’m having fun playing this character called Joshua.

Welcome to my world. Please handle with care.

# Cone of Shame

Hi friend,

My dog has the cone-of-shame on her.

You know – that cone that goes around the dog’s neck. 

She hates it.

I wish that I could tell her that it’s actually for her own good, to stop her from scratching her infection-filled ears.

Those puppy-dog eyes filled with sadness kill me\! 

I wish that I could tell her that it will all be over in just a few more days.

But there’s no way for me to communicate that with her, since we speak a different language.

So she just has to trust me.

Trust. 

What if when some challenge happens, we can learn to trust that it’s actually for our own good – and seek to find the value in it?

What if God, The Universe, Spirit, Mother Earth, Source – whatever you want to call it – is actually a well-intentioned master that is here to help us, but just speaks a different language?

Sometimes we need the cone to stop us from further hurting ourselves and allow the healing to happen.

Sometimes what feels like a restriction in the moment preventing us from getting what we think we want leads to something even better.  

Sometimes we get what we need.

In God We Trust,

Joshua “just a few more days” Church

# Dancing on the Edge of Destiny

She asks me,  
“What are we?”

And I laugh a little—  
not because it’s funny,  
but because how do you answer that  
without unspooling every lifetime we've lived?

How do I tell her  
that before we had names or faces,  
we circled this meeting on a soul map—  
said, “See you there,”  
and somehow,  
we didn’t forget.

But now, it feels like we’re playing charades,  
with God as the silent judge,  
guessing which lifetime we’ve rehearsed this in before—  
trying on the costumes:  
Lover,  
Mirror,  
Muse,  
Teacher,  
Partner,  
Friend.

What are we here for, really?

Did we come to burn like wildfire,  
leaving constellations of ash and memory?  
Or to build temples out of laughter and long nights,  
where we prayed with our eyes open?

Did we sign up to hold hands through the mystery,  
or to let go—  
just to prove that love doesn’t vanish  
when form changes its mind?

Maybe we came for this exact trembling—  
this sacred uncertainty—  
the space between inhale and exhale,  
where free will and fate dance cheek to cheek.

I can see God on the edge of his seat,  
popcorn refilled,  
wondering which timeline we’ll choose this time.

Maybe our only assignment  
was to break each other’s hearts wide open,  
so light could pour through the cracks,  
like sunrise through stained glass—  
reminding us:  
we are not here to own love,  
but to remember it.

Maybe we came here  
to learn what love looks like  
when it’s brave enough  
to not need an outcome.

To love as a verb, not a verdict.  
To bless what could be,  
and what never will be,  
with the same open palm.

So when she asks again,  
“What are we?”

I just smile and say,  
“Maybe we’re the lesson  
love wrote for itself  
to remember how to keep dancing  
on the edge of destiny.”

 across the linen sea.

 his reply, a low ripple beneath it.

 Wax sighs down the spine of time,

# Am I Awake? 

\*Spoken word poem written after my Junior Year Compartment Syndrome injury. Scan QR code for a link to the video of me performing the spoken word.\*

Am I really awake?  
It’s the start of the beginning.  
It’s the start of a long journey of difference that induces me to question my previous perception of living inference.

Beep beep beep. Machines chug.  
Beep beep beep. I fill my lungs.

I fill my lungs and breathe, breathe the last conscious breath for hours I will take,  
Father I pray to you if not I wake, my soul do take.

But I will awake.  
I will become awake.

The crisp, clean coolness of the operating room now introduces itself to my brain.  
Trying to suppress my jumping nerves, I make no gain.

And as boiling hot intelligent liquid enters through my veins, I feel my tense anxious body begin to relax.  
My worries, my nerves, my anticipation begins to collapse.

And slowly, so slowly I feel myself crawling into this dark, yet somehow warm corner-less case.  
My eyelids are pulled down with weights.  
Inevitable sleep is now upon my face.

And as my body, my vessel rests – my mind, my soul, my spirit are sharpest and at best.

The doctor is an artist, Picasso, you see.  
He is carving and shaping correct form into my body, myself, and me.  
He cuts and weaves and sews so very precisely.  
Making meaning out of color on my canvas, recharging my battery.

My physical body is now in the midst of a repair.  
Fixing and improving to allow my real self to express through this vessel, shall I dare?

Beep beep beep.  
The machines get faster and my heart pounds it’s way back to how is should be–  
Beep beep beep.

I can feel the warmth rush back into my blood,

from my hair to my mind into the breath that scratches life into me like a quick flash flood.

I am awake.

Not just physically anymore.

Feeling my spirit rejuvenate itself with my vessel, this is what I have been waiting for.

It truly awakens me, the adversity I know lies ahead.  
I will not allow it to manipulate me. I will defeat my own enemy in my head.

Because while the artist in the material world rewired my complex being,  
I was an artist to myself, and my self’s well being.  
I became awake, reaching that moment of clarity,  
Understanding that the start of the beginning   
still lies ahead of me.

# The Boy At The Wheel 

I was sitting across from someone I love,  
 words caught in my throat,  
 when I felt it—  
 that familiar tug on the wheel.

Something small inside me  
 had climbed into the driver’s seat,  
 hands too young for the steering column,  
 feet dangling above the pedals,  
 but somehow—still in control.

I knew him.  
 The boy version of me.  
 The one who learned early  
 how to grab the levers,  
 how to keep us safe  
 by pulling strings from the shadows.

Then came his whisper:  
 “Don’t speak your truth. Don’t rock the boat.  
 If you do, they’ll leave.  
 And if they leave, you’ll be alone.”

That’s when I realized—  
 I wasn’t the one steering.  
 It was him.  
 The boy inside me,  
 doing the only thing he knew: survive.

So instead of wrestling him down,  
 I said gently,  
 “Hey—pull over. Let’s talk.”

He looked at me, wide-eyed,  
 still clutching the wheel like a lifeline.  
 “It’s not your fault,” I told him.

That made him pause.  
 He pulled over, hands frozen on the wheel,  
 like letting go might break him.

“When you were younger,” I continued,  
 “you made a decision—  
 to be liked.  
 Because somewhere along the way,  
 you picked up a story:  
 that if you weren’t winning on the field,  
 if your teachers didn’t nod,  
 if your classmates didn’t laugh,  
 if your parents didn’t smile…  
 you weren’t worthy of love.”

He looked at me like I was reading his diary.  
 Because I was.

“That story moved in without asking,” I said.  
“It carved its name into the doorframe,  
dragged its suitcases across the floor,  
rearranged the furniture at will,  
and stuffed your self-expression into boxes  
taped shut with silence  
marked in black Sharpie:  
DO NOT OPEN.

And over time,  
 it hardened the softest parts of you.  
 The silly putty that once stretched and bent with ease  
 was left out in the sun until it stiffened and cracked—  
 no longer able to hold joy,  
 forgetting what it felt like  
 to be shaped by wonder,  
 forgetting how to play.

It dammed up the river of emotions—  
 because big boys don’t cry.  
 It built a watchtower where no one could reach you,  
 stood guard through sleepless nights,  
 raised the drawbridge  
 over the moat around your heart.

It told you to collect gold stars  
 like rations for the winter.  
 Convinced you to fold yourself into origami—  
 again and again,  
 until you fit inside the smallest box  
 someone else could carry.

It even rewrote the dictionary,  
 switching the meanings  
 of safety and freedom,  
 love and approval,  
 worth and achievement.”

He shifted in the seat,  
 eyes darting down,  
 like he wanted to argue—  
 his heel tapping a nervous rhythm on the floor,  
 the same way he used to  
 when he was about to cry,  
 but refused to.

“Maybe it served you then,” I softened.  
 “Maybe it kept your head above water  
 when you didn’t yet know how to swim.  
 But now I see it for what it is:  
 a lifeboat made of paper,  
 a shelter made of glass.  
 armor stitched from cobwebs,  
 a wall of snow in springtime.  
 A survival mechanism,  
 not the truth.”

He swallowed hard,  
 tears trembling at the edge of his lashes  
 as he realizes what he has done.

“Listen,” I said,  
 “this isn’t about banishing you.  
 You weren’t wrong to be afraid.  
 You weren’t trying to hurt me.  
 You were doing the best you could  
 with what you had.”

His lip quivered.  
 His hands loosened on the wheel.

“Come on,” I told him.  
 “Slide over.”

I pulled the car gently into park,  
 took the keys from the ignition,  
 and then pulled him close—  
 into the hug that’s been waiting for decades,  
 the kind that doesn’t let go  
 until every knot in his shoulders unravels,  
 until every muscle remembers what it feels like  
 to finally be safe.

“You don’t have to white-knuckle the wheel anymore.  
 You don’t have to guard the gates.  
 You don’t have to measure my worth  
 in the reflection of someone else’s eyes.

Because I’ve learned,  
 I don’t need to be liked to be worthy.  
 I don’t need applause to belong.  
 I belong simply because life chose me.

My roots run deeper than approval, you see,  
 anchored in something no hand can give or take.  
 This life, my life, is no mistake.

A heart that beats louder than shame ever could,  
 love in my veins that transcends every “should.”

I was born worthy — in all ways,  
 and this I vow to remember always.

I am held by my mother, the earth.  
 Sheltered by my brother, the trees.  
 Comforted by my father, the sky.

And I am surrounded—  
 swimming in a sea of humans  
 who see me.  
 who love the bones of me,  
 even when I forget how to love them myself.”

By then, the boy’s tears had spilled.  
 And so had mine.

So I buckled him gently  
 into the passenger seat,  
 watched his shoulders soften,  
 his breath steady,  
 his eyes brighten with wonder once again.

“You can ride with me,” I tell him.  
 “Your giggles, your awe, your imagination—  
 they’re our compass now.

And now together,  
 we drive forward into lands unknown,  
 on a new road unfolding beneath us—  
 a road that was waiting  
 for us to finally take it.

And with his small hand resting in mine,  
 I whisper:

Thank you.  
 I love you.  
 I’ve got it from here.

# Beginner Eyes

I called an Uber the other day.

Totally normal…  
until it wasn’t.

The moment after I pushed the big button on my phone and a driver was en-route 2 minutes away, something hit me.

I have a magic wand\!

A real magic wand, in my pocket.  
One command and a private driver is on his way to pick me up and take me anywhere   
	I want to go.  
One “Lumos” spell and just like Harry Potter I have a flashlight at the tip of my finger.  
One push of my thumb and I have instant access to all of the world’s information.  
One tap of my finger and I’m having a face to face conversation with someone anywhere  	in the world in real time.  
One post and I’m sharing something that can to reach over HALF of our world’s	population  
One string of words together and I have the ability to drastically affect the way that someone thinks and feels.

How cool is it to see my iPhone in this magical light?  
How cool is it to see the WORLD this way?  
Where else is there magic like this that I might be missing?

In that moment as I stepped into the Toyota Prius, an avalanche of invigorating thoughts swept my legs from under me.

Somewhere along the way,

We   
Forget.

Not only do we forget that we have a magic wand in our pockets called an iPhone –   
we forget how incredibly miraculous everything is –   
happening right   
in   
front   
of   
our   
eyes.

We forget.  
We forget that sometimes there’s colorful beams of light shooting across the sky that we’ve reduced to calling rainbows.  
RAINBOWS\!  
We forget that caterpillars wrap themselves into a cocoon and transform into a butterfly. What.  
We forget that the waves of the ocean have never stopped crashing to meet the shore. Literally ever.  
We forget that the local rock in orbit we call the moon is responsible for this.  
The moon.   
We forget that there used to be dinosaurs on our planet.   
Probably in the place you are now.  
We forget that trees are alive and breathing.   
And their breath keeps us alive.  
We forget that our bodies are made of remnants of stars.   
You are literally a star.  
We forget that there are airplanes flying through the sky and satellites orbiting our globe.  
Not to mention all the birds.  
We forget that we are a floating on a rock in space, spinning around 930 miles per hour  
Yeah, that’s fast.  
We forget that we’re all on the same team here, people. Human beings, doing our thing for the first time.  
And we’re doing our best.  
We forget how spectacular it is to be alive\!

There is a concept in Zen Buddhism known as \*\*shoshin\*\*, which means “beginner's mind.”

Seeing and experiencing things, as if for the first time…  
A baby elated by the plush feeling of the fuzzy carpet.  
A toddler shouting in ecstasy racing away from the crashing waves at the beach.  
A kid absorbed in awe by the countless colors of the setting sun.  
A teenager driving alone for the first time after getting their license.  
A father holding his child for the first time  
A mother meeting her grandchild for the first time.

It seemed as a kid we were obsessed with all of the magic that was around us, but we’ve become numb to it.  
Don’t forget that this world is a blooming paradise of splendor. You are a phenomenally powerful and essentially integral part of this gigantic, confusing, brutal, and awe inspiring world.  
Wipe the crust away from those beginner eyes and get up off the sidelines and tap into this jungle of magic.

Oh, and don’t forget your wand. 

# 30,000 Feet of Almost

She orders the coffee.  
 I’d read somewhere that the airplane pots almost never get cleaned,  
 and for a second I think about passing—  
 but she says yes,  
 so I say yes too.  
 Because apparently my faith in love  
 is stronger than my fear of bacteria.

The flight attendant hands us each a cup of steaming optimism,  
 thin as courage and twice as bitter,  
 and a pair of Biscoff cookies—  
 two little bricks of comfort that somehow taste like  
 childhood, safety, and mild flirtation.

We clink them together,  
 a toast to nowhere in particular,  
 and she laughs the way people do when the world feels smaller than it should.  
 I tell her I love that sound. She says, “what sound?”  
 And I don’t have the heart to tell her I meant *her voice.*

Somewhere over Nebraska, she falls asleep against the window,  
 and I start writing stories in my head about the life we could live  
 if seat 14A and 14B was titled Chapter 1\.

Our love would be patient, imperfect—  
 the kind where she forgets to close the kitchen cabinets  
 and I pretend it doesn’t bother me until one day it does.  
 We’d argue about nothing—  
 how she talks during movies, how I disappear into my thoughts—  
 and then somehow end up tangled on the couch,  
 apologizing with our hands.  
 She’d drink her coffee too sweet,  
 leave little notes in jacket pockets,  
 Belt out songs I don’t know in the shower  
 until they become the soundtrack of my mornings.

The captain says we’re descending soon.  
 The seatbelt sign flashes on like reality’s alarm clock.  
 I take a last sip—lukewarm, metallic,  
 and somehow holy.  
 She opens her eyes, smiles sleepily,  
 and I know this will probably end  
 the way airplane stories always do—  
 with polite goodbyes and a shared moment that never leaves you.

Still, as we touch down,  
 I swear my heart does that stupid thing again—  
 skips, stumbles,  
 stands up, brushes itself off,  
 and whispers,  
 “maybe next flight.”

# Acquainted Awareness

I stop and take a deep breath of air, sipping in intention on this glorious day.  
I fade in and out of consciousness, physically, that is as my spiritual self awakens in a pendulum response.  
I feel the hundreds – no – thousands of the sun’s sharp rays strike down and ignite my sleepy senseless self with such bright light.

The paddle board I lay restfully on, smoothly sways me back and forth lullabying me deeper into this dazed, yet acutely aware, state of being.

My eyelids gently cover the windows to my soul, yet they are as light as a feather – my soul is as open as it will ever be.  
The sound of the board gently colliding with the soft lake waves, mixed with the slow and steady beat of my heart scores the soundtrack of this soulful scene.  
The heat radiating from the summer sun encourages a drop of sweat to splatter onto my chest which is bashfully kissed by her throned rays.

I flop my numbed limb over as it plunges into the cool crisp water.

The innate response welcomes a chill to crawl up my spine, freezing the sweat, and erecting the hair on my arm.  
Peace, serenity, calm, and hakuna matata leisurely stroll through my mind.  
Nothing more, nothing less.  
I stare up into the sky with a soft yet fixed gaze, opening up my physical, emotional, mental, and etheric body.

In an eternal instant, light floods in and I become enveloped by the environment around me.

The world – our planet – becomes more than a mere inert object of mineral and mass.   
It becomes a living, sentient being.   
It is Divine Mother introducing herself to me, in one of her most powerful forms of manifestation.  

She wraps me with her embrace.   
Holding me with the sincerity of a mother holding her child for the first time.   
Smiling softly at the innocence of his returned gaze.   
Holding space for the curiosity of his delicate mind’s attempt at comprehending the fullness of the surrounding environment.

I inhale certainty, a combustion of brilliant light and electric love.  
And exhale relief, a new awareness of something I have known all along. 

Meeting an old friend in this new place.

I stop, take a long breath of air—  
 slow, deliberate, full of quiet intention.  
 The morning stretches open before me,  
 a gentle canvas of light and possibility.  
 For a moment, I fade in and out of form,  
 as if my body can’t decide  
 whether to stay or dissolve.

My spirit wakes like a pendulum,  
 swinging between dream and daylight.  
 The sun scatters thousands of golden arrows,  
 piercing through the stillness  
 to remind me what it feels like to be alive.

I am stretched out on a paddleboard,  
 floating somewhere between earth and sky.  
 The lake rocks me gently—  
 a mother’s hand on my back,  
 a lullaby for the soul.

My eyelids close,  
 light as feathers.  
 My soul, wide open.

The soft clap of water against board  
 and the slow percussion of my heartbeat  
 score this sacred scene.

The heat from the sun pulls a drop of sweat  
 from beneath my collarbone.  
 It travels down my chest  
 and meets her rays mid-journey—  
 a tender kiss from something infinite.

I let my arm fall into the lake,  
 feel the shock of coolness  
 climb my spine like a secret,  
 freezing the sweat,  
 raising the hairs on my arm  
 like tiny antennas reaching toward God.

Peace. Serenity. Calm.  
 They drift through my mind—

# Oh, Brother

“Please keep walking, please keep walking, please keep walking.” I murmured to myself.

Sitting in the aisle seat of an airplane during boarding, I was playing that game of judging who I wanted to be my seatmate for the flight. I watched as a very large overweight person shuffled down the aisle of the airplane during boarding, hoping – like the rest of the passengers near me – that he would walk past my seat.

As he approached, he wiped his sweaty brow with his arm and looked up at the seat numbers, then down at his boarding pass, then back up. 

He looked to be young, probably early 20’s. His big headphones sat around his neck, covered slightly by his long blonde hair which wet at the tips from sweat. 

He breathed heavily and pointed to the open middle seat next to me, looking at his boarding pass, indicating this was his seat. 

Crap.

I stood up and stepped back as he made his way into the seat, leveraging his full weight on the seat backs in front of him rocking the passengers in those seats. For the next 8 hours on our international flight to Iceland, this was my seatmate. 

Inner judgments filled my head. I was hoping I would sit next to someone I could have a cool conversation with or learn something from. Or someone who would be a very small human so I could comfortably write in my journal or work on my computer without having to fight over the armrest. With this guy next to me, there was no arm rest. He had to put it up on both sides just to fit in the seat. 

I gave him a moment to settle in and then sat back down. Now the judgements were spiraling out of control. How could someone let themselves get so overweight? Why did it have to be ME he’s sitting next to? 

I grew frustrated as not only was there no way I could write in my journal or work on my computer, but even worse – there was no way that I could sit without having our arms fully touching. I just put on some music and closed my eyes as they closed the boarding doors. 

And right on time, the miracle happened. The insight came through, my perspective shifted.. 

This kid was about my younger brother's age. I thought about it for a second and realized that this kid may very likely have an older brother somewhere, who loves him dearly like I love my younger brother. And how upset I would be if people were judging him the way I was judging him. 

The tenseness in my shoulders began to relax as the feeling of compassion began to wash over me. 

The insight nudged a step further. Not only could this be my little brother – this IS my little brother. After all, we are all part of the same human race navigating life on earth together. We’re all on the same team.

As I sat with this thought and embodied this perspective my reality began to shift. His arm didn’t bother me any more. I relaxed into the seat and focused on my breath and this expanding feeling of connectivity to the humans around me on this tin can now jetting through the sky at 500 mph.

I am right where I need to be. 

**⚡Takeaways:**

1. **Todos Es Médecine**  
   A few years ago I did a vision quest in the desert – doing a water fast for 2 days isolated in the elements with nothing around, not even a tent. A poignant takeaway I walked away from was something the shaman said – “Todos Es Médecine.” “Everything is medicine.” We are always given what we need to heal, even if that thing may not be what we want it to be or expect it to be. This was a total reinforcement of that. The exact medicine I needed was the very thing I didn’t want, but it led to this paradigm shifting insight. who was leading that experience  doing a vision quest, everything is medicine. Everyone carries a piece of the puzzle. There’s no exception to this perspective. The universe doesn’t miss. This MUST be the person I need to sit next to. There must be something here for me.   
2. **Choose Compassion**  
   Our judgments say more about ourselves than they do others. Notice your judgments. How can you shift the judgment to a talk track of compassion? The person you are judging is someone’s hero in their life. They are another human being equally deserving of love and compassion as you are. We really are all on one team. I wonder what would happen if everyone just for a day truly did view their neighbors and people they interact with as a brother or sister? My invitation to you is to go through your day and practice embodying this perspective and see how your interactions may shift.   
3. **Our Perceptions Shift Reality**   
   Our perceptions can drastically change our reality, and in my opinion – this is TRUE magic\! On the plane, one minute I’m frustrated and negative and the next I’m compassionate and calm and understanding. Nothing changed besides the story I was telling myself. I was still in the same seat, still touching arms with this guy but the entire situation had changed. This is the power that our inner dialogue and our perspectives have on us. One thing I’ve learned is that when we feel the negativity it means we are looking through a doorway that is not aligned with our inner knowing and higher self. When the story feels good, it means it is aligned. We can use our emotions as our GPS to help us choose the right door to walk down, the right lens in which to see a situation and therefore our world.

# Heads or Tails?

Hi friend,

"Just flip a coin."

Sound advice.

Not because it seemingly absolves you from making a decision on a subject.

But because the moment you flip the coin – you know.

As it starts spinning in what seems like slow motion you quietly start to cheer on a side.

When it lands on the ground (or when you flip it on your arm – I don't judge your flipping style) you are all but praying for it to be what you want.

That's the power of heads or tails.

Well, the real power is in your intuition.

It comes through so clearly in that moment.

There's no hiding from it. No faking it.

We have the ability to tune into this voice throughout the day when making decisions for ourselves.

And not all decisions are easy…

Do I get out of bed for my workout or keep sleeping?  
Do I eat that piece of cake?  
Do I go out with friends or stay in?  
Do I, do I, do I?

When you catch yourself looking around and trying to logically make a decision like this, just stop.

Stop and ask yourself one simple question…

"If I really loved myself what would I do?"

It's like flipping a coin for your intuition.

You know instantly. And there's no faking it.

Your intuition is the only one that knows if "I'm too tired to workout" is an excuse or you truly need some rest.

Your intuition is the only one that knows whether you will feel good about eating the cake or be mad at yourself later.

Your intuition is the only one that knows if you will feel energized by staying in or stagnant.

Your intuition knows. YOU know.

A few more juicy questions to chew on in your journal on a regular or daily basis…

1. What’s on your mind / what feels heavy?  
2. What (or who) are you grateful for? (and FEEL that gratitude)  
3. How did God interact with you today?  
4. What do you need to know?

So let me ask you..

What would YOU do if YOU really loved yourself??

Loving myself,
